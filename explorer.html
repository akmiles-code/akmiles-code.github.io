Chemistry rules: ω-ester crosslinks form between Ser/Thr (hydroxyl nucleophile) and Asp/Glu (carboxylate electrophile, ATP-grasp activated), loss of H₂O = −18.011 Da each. ω-amide (lactam) crosslinks form between Lys ε-amine and Asp/Glu, also −18.011 Da. Topology constraints: non-crossing (rings may be sequential or nested, not interleaved); each residue used at most once. Scoring is heuristic based on ring size, sequence position, and local context — not a structural prediction. ETD fragment masses are monoisotopic. Cyclic boundary ions appear at the c-ion where both crosslinked residues first appear in the same N-terminal fragment (ring closed = −18.011 Da shift).
